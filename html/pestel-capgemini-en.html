Capgemini: PESTEL analysis

Opportunities

Political

  • Will of the State to accelerate the digital transformation of France.

Economic

  • Significant economic growth in Asian countries with strong digital transformation.

Socio-cultural

  • Advocate gender equality in technology.
  • Promote different ethnicities.
  • Inclusion of people with disabilities.
  • Tendency of generation X to begin to understand and use computers.
  • Lifestyle more and more turned to digital and the ease it provides.

Technological

  • Ease of access to growing technologies.
  • Need for publishers that their software packages are integrated into organizations.
  • Acceleration of digital transformation following the pandemic.

Ecological

  • Promoting an environmentally friendly business.
  • Adopt the most economical production possible in order to sow competition.

Threats

Political

  • Compliance with competition law.
  • Ever more demanding regulations concerning the protection of personal data.
  • Regulations concerning the protection of minors on the Internet.
  • Impact of Brexit on the UK and European market.

Economic

  • Following the economic crisis linked to Covid 19, banks tend to be more reluctant to lend large sums, in particular related to little-known projects.
  • Due to the evolution of interest rates, many companies are experiencing slowdowns economics which then cause the value of their shares to fall.
  • Impact of the war in Ukraine on business operations.

Socio-cultural

  • New mentalities vis-à-vis equal pay and qualification between men and women.
  • Moral requirements of the inclusion of people of all nationalities, of any validity, of any sex.
  • Preference to have fun “in the open air”. People often want to take a break from the world grabbing computer science.

Technological

  • Potential slowdown in investments in the field of information technology, the latter having experienced significant growth over the past 30 years.

Ecological

  • Dramatic increase in energy costs following the scarcity of resources.
  • State measures aimed at limiting energy consumption.
  • Measures taken against the emission of greenhouse gases, forcing multinationals to do the same. Facilities must become less energy-intensive and less polluting.
