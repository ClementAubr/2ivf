A quick presentation

Capgemini is a multinational technology, management and outsourcing consulting firm. It was founded in 1967 in France and today has more than 270,000 employees in more than 50 countries. Capgemini offers a range of services, particularly in terms of strategy consulting, consulting, digital, technology and outsourcing. The company has offices in many countries, including France, in Germany, the United States and India.

Management areas

Supply

Since Capgemini offers services, it does not need to source materials first. However, partnerships are at the heart of its activity since it is responsible for configuring for customers software created by software publishers. In this way, Capgemini counts no less than twenty major partnerships including Adobe, , IBM, Google Cloud or Amazon

Production

The most requested service at Capgemini is outsourcing, consisting of administering and ensuring the sustainability of all or part of a company's digital information system. This translates in particular by Third-Party Application Maintenance, i.e. making sure to keep the software packages in an optimal state of operation, even if it means taking responsibility for any problems. services are often applied via 3 to 5 year contracts with the company in question.

Capgemini also offers software integration by implementing and configuring software packages so that they best correspond to what the company expects. This task is generally remunerated at the mission ; mission that can last several months or even a year depending on the complexity of the settings. Finally, the firm offers training in the use of digital tools as well as advice in the domain.

Commercial

Capgemini segments its customer base into 2 main types of customers: companies that are interested by software integration or maintenance services, and those seeking advice to improve their productivity thanks to digital technology. The targeting is thus differentiated: one will receive outsourcing while the other will be guided by Capgemini's consulting firm.

Capgemini is targeting a large clientele, with a range of prices that varies according to complexity of work demand. All companies requiring its services are concerned, whether they are small or large.

Human Resources Management

Capgemini is a company in tune with the times, which tries in particular to evolve towards an equality of sexes. Thus, it promotes job and salary parity with the objective that 30% of jobs positions will be held by women by 2025. In addition, Capgemini is part of the Valuable 500, a global network of leaders involved in the inclusion of people with disabilities, with appropriate infrastructure.

Regarding salaries, employees are well paid with salaries ranging from €19,500 for a new recruit, to €57,000 net per year for an experienced project manager. The working conditions offered are pleasant: dynamic and professional atmosphere, working hours flexible and able to telecommute. Employees have 25 to 33 days off per year according to their seniority.

Finance

Shareholders constitute an important part of the financing of the company. Thus, Capgemini's total capital is made up of 0.2% self-financing, 6.2% shareholders individuals and 85% institutional investors. Among these major players, 67.5% are based in the international. Finally, 8.6% of the capital is held by the group's employees.

Nevertheless, at the end of 2021, Capgemini had approximately €3.224 billion debt, i.e. 37.4% of the company's equity - the latter amounting to €8.48 billion. This remains decent for a company the size of Capgemini. Today, the company continues to diversify and expand. The latest major friendly takeover is that of Altran in 2018, specialized in engineering consulting, and renamed Capgemini Engineering.

Accounting

As required by law, the company's balance sheets are transparent and available directly on their official website. Capgemini regularly prepares and publishes a provisional calendar reflecting the next major events of the company. Thus, on the third quarter of 2022, Capgemini pocketed nearly 5,553 million euros and has a growth rate of 15.7%.

In 2021, it generated a turnover of 16.8 billion euros. From different markets in which Capgemini is present, France represents nearly 22% of its turnover business, and North America contributes 32%, testifying to its global reach.

Research

Capgemini advocates digital innovation and it is therefore essential to carry out Research work. It undertakes many works aimed at improving and making more complete its services like its AI. Likewise, new technologies are appearing all the time and it is important for Capgemini to adapt to it.

For example, a software package might start offering a new functionality, which Capgemini will have to learn to use, implement and configure in the way more optimal. It is a work of constant learning and optimization. The company highlights 6 main sectors in which it is very active and which it hopes revolutionize. These are the mobility and IT of the future, health, industry, data science and machine learning.
